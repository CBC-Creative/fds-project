    Indoor Air Quality Monitoring Is Fundamental To An Effective Clean Air Strategy.

    After all, without accurate and actionable data on the air quality of a space, it is impossible to understand what pollutants are present, their concentrations, and how they are affecting your overall air quality.

    Consistent indoor air quality monitoring is recommended by the EPA1 to help create and maintain optimum indoor air quality. By implementing effective monitoring technology, you gain clarity and control of the actual air quality status across all of your spaces, and are empowered to take immediate, precise, and effective steps to protect the wellbeing of your occupants.

    1 https://www.epa.gov/indoor-air-quality-iaq
    IAQ monitoring should become the new norm in every building. In the same way we measure the temperature in buildings, the IAQ can be monitored with real-time sensor technology.
    Dr. Joseph Allen, Associate Professor at Harvard;
    Director of Harvard Healthy Buildings Program
    Presenting at the White House Summit on Indoor Air Quality (Oct. 2022)
    Many Reasons to Monitor
    With Fellowes Indoor Air Quality Monitoring, Building Owners And Engineers Can Make Advancements Towards The Workplace Metrics That Matter Most.

    Protect Occupant
    Health & Wellness

    Effective indoor air quality monitoring can reduce or eliminate occupants' exposure to dangerous contaminants for better short-term and long-term wellness.

    Reduce Building
    Operating Costs

    35% of building energy usage comes from HVAC. Indoor air quality monitoring enables leaders to deploy targeted, localized air purification interventions with greater efficiency and cost control.

    Earn Points Towards
    Green Building Certifications

    By meeting recommended standards of indoor air purification and sustainability, organizations can make substantial gains towards Green Building certifications and other public health initiatives.
    The Benefits of Monitoring

    Every Space Is Unique. And Every Space Faces Its Own Air Quality Challenges.

    Despite the impressive efficiency and performance of modern HVAC equipment, these systems are not designed to provide localized, space-by-space data on air quality. As a result, pollutants can collect and linger in specific areas -- undetected by conventional HVAC sensors.

    By implementing building-wide air monitoring, organizational leaders have accurate, actionable data on the most important air quality metrics that shape the health and enjoyment of their spaces.

    Effective Indoor Air Quality Monitoring Collects Real-Time Data On The Presence And Concentrations Of Common Indoor Air Contaminants.

    Fellowes Array air purifiers and monitors measure the following:

    Particulate Matter
    PM1, PM2.5, PM10

    Some particles less than 10 microns in diameter can get deep into your lungs and some may even get into your bloodstream. Particles less than 2.5 microns (PM2.5) pose the greatest risk to health.

    Total Volatile Organic
    Compounds (TVOCs)

    Chemical-derived gases emitted from solids and liquids such as cleaning products and furniture that contribute to sensory irritation, headaches, and even organ damage.

    Carbon Dioxide
    (CO2)

    The by-product of human respiration and combustion of fossil fuels. CO2 has been linked to reduced mental faculties, headache, dizziness, and fatigue.

    Air Pressure

    Air pressure affects air circulation and can degrade IAQ in buildings. Monitoring pressure levels helps identify ventilation issues.

    Ambient Temperature

    Despite central HVAC, space to temperature variation is common and can lead to personal discomfort, loss of morale, and frustration.

    Humidity Levels

    High humidity levels can cause occupant discomfort and create favorable conditions for mold and bacterial growth.

    Certifications like WELL, LEED, fitwel and RESET guide building owners in creating healthier, more sustainable environments. Achieving these certifications demonstrates a commitment to health, wellness, and safety in buildings and each place great importance on the ability to monitor IAQ.
