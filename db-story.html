Family

1917

When a young, entrepreneurial Harry Fellowes sold his first Bankers Box record solution in the early 20th century, he understood immediately that as useful as quality products might be, people were ultimately looking to solve problems that made their work difficult, time-consuming, or inefficient.

With this humble, yet profound understanding, Harry set out to build a company that was not only uniquely capable of solving those problems, but grounded in a set of deeply-held values that remain part of the Fellowes culture to this day.

1931-1936

Building the Banker’s Box Brand

1931-1936

The Bankers Box Company grew quickly as businesses around the country sought easy, reliable tools to maintain organized record storage. In 19XX, Harry decided it was time to allow the next generation to lead the organization, and handed the Bankers Box company down to his two sons, John and Folger Fellowes.

John and Folger led the business through immense growth periods, establishing Bankers Box as a household name. As the 1920s came to a close, the two brothers skillfully led the company through the Great Depression, building a strong financial foundation driven by sound business practices and continuous improvement.

1941

Supporting the War Effort

As the nation was engulfed in World War 2, the Bankers Box Company found an unexpected advantage from building their business around paper-based storage solutions.

Because the United States was working to conserve metals for the war effort, Bankers Boxes experienced even more demand from businesses and people around the country.

1980 - 1990

Expanding to Business Machines

Early in 1982, Fellowes entered a licensing agreement with a German shredder manufacturing facility, allowing the organization to transition into the rapidly-developing product sector of business machines.

In addition to growing the brand’s manufacturing prowess, the move aided the company’s vision to deliver a wider range of products that enable people to be more productive and efficient.
