    AIR CONCERNS FOR YOUR SCHOOL

    Experts agree: airborne transmission of the virus poses a significant threat.
    “CDC says airborne transmission plays a role in coronavirus spread.”
    Washington Post, October 5, 2020
    “The virus can remain suspended in the air for up to 3 hours.”
    New England Journal of Medicine, May 14, 2020
    “It is time to address airborne transmission of COVID-19.”
    An open letter to the WHO from 239 Scientists, July 6, 2020
    Healthy Air in Schools
    Classrooms
    • Classrooms
    • Nurses Offices
    • Teachers Lounges
    • Administrative Offices

    Classrooms and lecture halls are essential areas for virus protection where students might not be able to properly social distance, and conversation is key to learning. Air purification helps remove these concerns around student and staff safety, while also benefiting lungs by eliminating allergens and odors.

    Key Concerns:
    Viruses
    Allergens
    Odors

    In nurses offices where students gather when sick, the threat of airborne particles lingering in the air for up to 3 hours poses a further risk between student visits. Increased air changes helps to remove these airborne particles, and Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) that may be emitted due to increased cleaning measures.

    Key Concerns:
    Allergens
    Viruses
    VOCs

    Where teachers and staff gather between classes and for lunch, virus protection remains steadily important. In these spaces, unpleasant odors from lunches, snacks, garbage and more may linger in the air. Eliminate these unpleasant odors by increased air exchanges.

    Key Concerns:
    Allergens
    Viruses
    Odors
    VOCs

    In administrative offices, where staff spend a majority of their time, it’s important to continue to protect them against the airborne transmission of viruses. Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) are also an area of concern, as harmful chemicals are emitted from office equipment to cleaning supplies.

    Key Concerns:
    Allergens
    Viruses
    Odors
    VOCs
