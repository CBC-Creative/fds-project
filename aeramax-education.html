Classrooms and lecture halls are essential areas for virus protection where students might not be able to properly social distance, and conversation is key to learning. Air purification helps remove these concerns around student and staff safety, while also benefiting lungs by eliminating allergens and odors.

In nurses offices where students gather when sick, the threat of airborne particles lingering in the air for up to 3 hours poses a further risk between student visits. Increased air changes helps to remove these airborne particles, and Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) that may be emitted due to increased cleaning measures.

Where teachers and staff gather between classes and for lunch, virus protection remains steadily important. In these spaces, unpleasant odors from lunches, snacks, garbage and more may linger in the air. Eliminate these unpleasant odors by increased air exchanges.
