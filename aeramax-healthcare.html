    “The virus can remain suspended in the air for up to 3 hours.”

    “It is time to address airborne transmission of COVID-19.”

    Healthy air for the whole facility

    WAITING ROOMS
    • WAITING ROOMS
    • EXAMINATION ROOMS
    • BREAKROOMS
    • BATHROOMS

    Waiting rooms are a haven for pathogens, and unfortunately, those who are sick are mingling with those who aren’t. Not to forget putting at risk those who already have compromised immune systems. Air purification helps to remove the concern of spreading airborne contaminants in these close quarters.

    Key Concerns:
    Viruses
    Allergens
    Odors

    According to the CDC, one in 25 patients acquires an infection while in the hospital. That’s more than 700,000 patients per year, with 75,000 of those people dying because of it. It’s key to eliminate as many germs and bacteria from these health environments. Even more, in dental procedures utilizing high speed dental tools causes contaminants including fungi and bacteria to splatter the air.

    Key Concerns:
    Viruses
    Allergens
    Odors
    VOCs

    In areas where healthcare staff gather to enjoy lunch or breaks, it's important to protect against viruses. Moreover, prevent unpleasant odors from lunches, snacks, garbage and more that may linger in the air.

    Increasing air changes per hour helps eliminate unpleasant odors and viruses.
